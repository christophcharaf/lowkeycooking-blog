## Ingredients

- 1 lb ground beef (80/20 blend)
- 2 brioche buns, split
- 2 slices cheese (cheddar or American)
- 1 tbsp olive oil or butter
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

## Instructions

1. Divide the ground beef into two equal portions.
2. Heat the cast iron skillet over medium-high heat with olive oil or butter.
3. Place a portion of beef onto the hot skillet. Immediately place a piece of parchment paper over the beef and use a burger press or spatula to firmly smash it into a thin patty. Remove parchment paper.
4. Season the smashed patty generously with salt and pepper.
5. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the edges are very crispy and browned.
6. Flip the patty and immediately top with a slice of cheese.
7. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and the patty is cooked through.
8. Toast the brioche buns in the skillet for about 30 seconds to 1 minute.
9. Assemble the burgers: place the cooked patty on the bottom bun, add the top bun, and serve immediately.
