## Ingredients

- 4 cups fresh or frozen corn kernels (about 4-5 ears of corn)
- 1/4 cup whole milk
- 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted, plus more for cooking
- 8 ounces queso de mano, or substitute with mozzarella or fresh ricotta cheese

## Instructions

1. If using fresh corn, carefully remove the kernels from the cobs. If using frozen corn, ensure it is thawed.
2. In a blender, combine the corn kernels, milk, sugar, salt, and 2 tablespoons of melted butter. Blend until the mixture is mostly smooth but still retains a slight texture from the corn. Avoid over-blending to prevent a gummy texture.
3. Transfer the corn batter to a mixing bowl. Stir in the all-purpose flour until just combined. Let the batter rest for 5-10 minutes.
4. Heat a large non-stick skillet or griddle over medium heat. Lightly grease the surface with a small amount of butter or cooking oil.
5. Pour about 1/4 to 1/3 cup of batter for each cachapa onto the hot skillet, spreading it gently with the back of the ladle to form a pancake approximately 4-5 inches in diameter.
6. Cook for 3-4 minutes per side, or until each cachapa is golden brown and cooked through. The edges should appear set.
7. Once the first side is cooked and you've flipped the cachapa, immediately place a slice or a small handful of queso de mano (or your chosen cheese) on one half of the cachapa.
8. Cook for another 1-2 minutes, then gently fold the cachapa in half over the cheese. Continue to cook until the cheese is melted and warm.
9. Remove the cooked cachapa from the skillet and keep warm while you cook the remaining batter. Serve immediately, perhaps with a dollop of nata (Venezuelan cream) if desired.
