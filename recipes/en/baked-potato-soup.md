## Ingredients

- 2 lbs Russet potatoes, peeled and diced into 1/2-inch cubes
- 6 slices bacon, chopped
- 4 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 2 cups whole milk
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 1 1/2 cups shredded sharp cheddar cheese, plus more for garnish
- 1/2 tsp salt, or to taste
- 1/4 tsp black pepper, or to taste
- 2 green onions, sliced, for garnish

## Instructions

1.  In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook the chopped bacon over medium heat until crispy. Remove bacon with a slotted spoon and set aside on a paper towel-lined plate. Reserve 2 tablespoons of bacon grease in the pot.
2.  Add butter to the pot with the bacon grease. Once melted, add the chopped yellow onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
3.  Sprinkle the flour over the onion mixture and whisk constantly for 1-2 minutes to create a roux.
4.  Gradually whisk in the chicken broth, then slowly add the milk, stirring continuously to prevent lumps. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
5.  Add the diced potatoes to the pot. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until potatoes are fork-tender.
6.  Using a potato masher or the back of a spoon, gently mash about half of the potatoes against the side of the pot to thicken the soup to your desired consistency.
7.  Remove the pot from the heat. Stir in the sour cream and 1 1/2 cups of shredded cheddar cheese until melted and smooth.
8.  Season the soup with salt and black pepper to taste.
9.  Ladle the hot soup into bowls. Garnish each serving with reserved crispy bacon, extra shredded cheddar cheese, and sliced green onions. Serve immediately.
