## Ingredients

- 2 cooked lobsters (about 1.5 lbs each), meat removed and bodies reserved
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 large onion, chopped
- 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
- 2 celery stalks, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 cup brandy (optional)
- 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
- 6 cups lobster or seafood stock
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley, for garnish
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

## Instructions

1. Crack lobster bodies to expose shells. In a large Dutch oven, melt butter over medium heat. Add lobster bodies and cook, stirring occasionally, until shells are bright red and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
2. Add onion, carrots, and celery to the pot. Cook, stirring, until vegetables are softened, about 8-10 minutes. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute more until fragrant.
3. If using brandy, carefully pour it into the pot and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom. Let it bubble and reduce slightly for about 1 minute.
4. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and lobster shells. Stir well to coat and cook for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly, to form a roux.
5. Gradually whisk in the lobster or seafood stock, ensuring no lumps form. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce heat to low, cover, and cook for 20 minutes to allow flavors to meld.
6. Remove the lobster shells from the pot with a slotted spoon. Discard shells. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean pot, pressing down on solids to extract maximum liquid. Discard solids.
7. Return the strained broth to low heat. Stir in the heavy cream and lobster meat. Cook gently until the bisque is heated through, but do not boil.
8. Use an immersion blender to purée the bisque until smooth and creamy. Alternatively, carefully transfer batches to a regular blender and purée.
9. Season the bisque with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
10. Ladle the hot bisque into bowls. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately.
