## Ingredients

- 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas, rinsed and drained (reserve 1/4 cup liquid)
- 1/2 cup tahini
- 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (from 1-2 lemons)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste
- 2-4 tablespoons ice water, or reserved chickpea liquid
- 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for serving
- Pinch of paprika or sumac, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions

1. Drain the can of chickpeas, reserving about 1/4 cup of the liquid. Rinse the drained chickpeas thoroughly under cold water.
2. In a food processor, combine the tahini and lemon juice. Process for 1-2 minutes until thick and creamy, scraping down the sides as needed.
3. Add the minced garlic, ground cumin, and salt to the food processor. Process for another 30 seconds.
4. Add the rinsed chickpeas to the food processor. Process for 3-5 minutes, scraping down the sides frequently, until the mixture is very smooth.
5. With the food processor running, slowly drizzle in 2-4 tablespoons of ice water (or reserved chickpea liquid) until the hummus reaches your desired creamy consistency. It should be light and fluffy.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed, adding more salt or lemon juice if desired.
7. Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl. Drizzle generously with olive oil and garnish with a pinch of paprika or sumac, if using.
8. Serve immediately with pita bread, fresh vegetables, or as a spread, or refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 5 days.
