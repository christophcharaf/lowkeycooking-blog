## Ingredients

- 2 lbs (907g) cream cheese, softened
- 1 ½ cups (300g) granulated sugar
- 4 large eggs, room temperature
- 2 cups (480ml) heavy cream, room temperature
- 1 tablespoon ceremonial grade matcha powder
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- ½ teaspoon salt
- Parchment paper

## Instructions

1.  Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a 9-inch springform pan with two sheets of parchment paper, crinkling them and pressing them into the pan so the paper extends several inches above the rim.
2.  In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and granulated sugar with an electric hand mixer on medium speed until smooth and creamy, about 2-3 minutes. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed.
3.  Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition until just combined. Be careful not to overmix.
4.  In a separate small bowl, whisk together the heavy cream, matcha powder, vanilla extract, and salt until the matcha is fully dissolved and there are no lumps. You can use a fine-mesh sieve to sift the matcha into the cream for a smoother mixture.
5.  Pour the matcha-cream mixture into the cream cheese batter and mix on low speed until just combined and smooth. Do not overmix; overmixing can incorporate too much air, leading to cracks.
6.  Pour the batter into the prepared springform pan. Gently tap the pan on the counter a few times to release any large air bubbles.
7.  Bake for 45-55 minutes, or until the top is deeply caramelized and almost burnt-looking, and the edges are set but the center still has a slight jiggle. The cheesecake will puff up significantly.
8.  Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool completely at room temperature. It will deflate as it cools.
9.  Once cooled, transfer the cheesecake to the refrigerator and chill for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, before serving.
10. To serve, remove the springform pan ring and carefully peel away the parchment paper. Slice and enjoy!
