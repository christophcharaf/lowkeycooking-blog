## Ingredients

- 24 oz (3 blocks) full-fat cream cheese, softened
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 4 large eggs, room temperature
- 2 cups heavy cream
- 2 tbsp culinary grade matcha powder
- 1 tbsp all-purpose flour
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/4 tsp salt

## Instructions

1.  Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a 9-inch springform pan with two sheets of parchment paper, allowing it to crinkle up the sides and extend a few inches above the rim. This helps create the signature burnt edges and prevents sticking.
2.  In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and granulated sugar with an electric hand mixer on medium speed until smooth and creamy, about 2-3 minutes. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed.
3.  Add the eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition until just combined. Do not overmix, as too much air can cause cracks.
4.  In a separate medium bowl, whisk together the heavy cream and matcha powder until the matcha is fully dissolved and there are no lumps. You can use a fine-mesh sieve to sift the matcha into the cream for a smoother mixture.
5.  Pour the matcha cream mixture into the cream cheese mixture and mix on low speed until just combined and smooth.
6.  Sift the all-purpose flour into the batter, then add the vanilla extract and salt. Mix on low speed until the flour is fully incorporated and the batter is smooth. Be careful not to overmix.
7.  Pour the batter into the prepared springform pan. Gently tap the pan on the counter a few times to release any large air bubbles.
8.  Bake for 40-50 minutes, or until the top is deeply caramelized and dark brown, almost burnt, and the edges are set but the center still jiggles slightly when gently shaken.
9.  Remove the cheesecake from the oven and let it cool completely at room temperature in the pan. The center will sink as it cools.
10. Once cooled, transfer the cheesecake (still in the pan) to the refrigerator and chill for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, before serving.
11. When ready to serve, remove the cheesecake from the springform pan and carefully peel away the parchment paper. Slice and enjoy!
