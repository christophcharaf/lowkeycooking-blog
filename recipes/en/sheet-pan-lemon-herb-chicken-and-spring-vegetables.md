This sheet pan dinner is my go-to for busy weeknights when I want something healthy and delicious without a lot of fuss. The lemon and herbs infuse the chicken and vegetables with incredible flavor, making it feel like a restaurant-quality meal. The best part? Everything cooks on one pan, which means minimal cleanup, and that’s a win in my book! Even if you’re new to cooking, you’ll find this recipe incredibly straightforward and rewarding.

## Ingredients

- 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into 1.5-inch pieces
- 1 lb small red or yellow potatoes, quartered
- 1 lb asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 1 red onion, cut into wedges
- 1 lemon, half juiced, half cut into wedges for serving
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon dried oregano
- 1 tablespoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a large baking sheet with parchment paper for easier cleanup.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, minced garlic, oregano, thyme, salt, and pepper.
3. Add the chicken pieces, quartered potatoes, asparagus pieces, and red onion wedges to the bowl. Toss everything gently to ensure it's evenly coated with the marinade.
4. Arrange the coated chicken and vegetables in a single layer on the prepared baking sheet. Make sure not to overcrowd the pan; use two pans if necessary.
5. Roast for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through (internal temperature reaches 165°F/74°C) and the vegetables are tender and slightly caramelized.
6. Serve immediately, with fresh lemon wedges on the side for squeezing over the top.
