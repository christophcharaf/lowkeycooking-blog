## Ingredients

- 2 large eggs
- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
- Pinch of fine sea salt
- Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- 1 teaspoon fresh chives, finely chopped (optional, for garnish)

## Instructions

1.  Crack the eggs into a small mixing bowl. Add a pinch of salt and a grind of black pepper.
2.  Whisk the eggs vigorously with a fork or whisk until just combined and slightly frothy, but avoid incorporating too much air. You want a smooth, homogenous mixture.
3.  Heat a 6-8 inch non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the butter and swirl it around until it melts, foams, and just begins to brown slightly.
4.  Pour the egg mixture into the hot skillet. Immediately begin stirring the eggs with a rubber spatula in small, rapid circles for about 10-15 seconds, moving the cooked egg from the bottom to the top.
5.  Once the eggs are mostly set but still very moist and creamy on top, stop stirring. Gently shake the pan to ensure the omelette isn't sticking and the bottom is lightly golden.
6.  Tilt the pan slightly away from you. Using the spatula, gently fold one-third of the omelette from the edge closest to you towards the center. Then, fold the opposite edge over the first fold to create a classic three-fold shape.
7.  Carefully slide the omelette onto a warm plate, seam-side down. If desired, garnish with fresh chopped chives. Serve immediately.
