This Easy Strawberry Rhubarb Crumble is one of my go-to desserts, especially when rhubarb season hits. I love the perfect balance of sweet strawberries and tart rhubarb, all topped with a buttery, crunchy crumble that’s incredibly satisfying. It feels fancy, but I promise it’s so simple to make, even if you’re new to baking. You’ll have this delicious treat ready for any occasion with minimal fuss.

## Ingredients

- 4 cups fresh rhubarb, chopped into 1-inch pieces
- 3 cups fresh strawberries, hulled and halved (or quartered if large)
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar, divided
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch
- 1 teaspoon lemon zest
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 cup rolled oats (not instant)
- 1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/2 cup (1 stick) cold unsalted butter, cut into small cubes

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Lightly grease a 9-inch pie plate or an 8x8 inch baking dish.
2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the chopped rhubarb and strawberries.
3. Add 1/4 cup of the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and lemon zest to the fruit. Toss gently to coat the fruit evenly.
4. Pour the fruit mixture into the prepared baking dish and spread it out evenly.
5. In a separate medium bowl, combine the flour, rolled oats, brown sugar, cinnamon, and the remaining 1/2 cup of granulated sugar.
6. Add the cold, cubed butter to the dry ingredients. Use a pastry blender, your fingertips, or two forks to cut the butter into the dry ingredients until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs with some pea-sized pieces of butter remaining.
7. Sprinkle the crumble topping evenly over the fruit mixture in the baking dish.
8. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the topping is golden brown and the fruit is bubbly.
9. Let the crumble cool for at least 15-20 minutes before serving. This allows the filling to set up properly.
10. Serve warm, perhaps with a scoop of vanilla ice cream or a dollop of whipped cream.
