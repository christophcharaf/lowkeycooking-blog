## Ingredients

- 2 medium zucchini (about 1.5 lbs), grated
- 1 tsp salt, plus more to taste
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1 large egg, lightly beaten
- 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh parsley
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
- 1/4 cup olive oil or vegetable oil, for frying

## Instructions

1. Grate the zucchini using a box grater. Place the grated zucchini in a colander, sprinkle with 1 teaspoon of salt, and let it sit for 10-15 minutes to draw out excess moisture.
2. Transfer the salted zucchini to a clean kitchen towel or several layers of paper towels. Squeeze out as much liquid as possible. This step is crucial for crispy fritters.
3. In a large mixing bowl, combine the squeezed zucchini, all-purpose flour, beaten egg, chopped parsley, minced garlic, black pepper, baking powder, and grated Parmesan cheese (if using). Mix until just combined.
4. Heat the olive oil or vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. The oil should be hot enough that a drop of batter sizzles immediately.
5. Drop spoonfuls of the zucchini batter into the hot oil, flattening them slightly with the back of a spoon to form small patties, about 2-3 inches in diameter. Do not overcrowd the pan.
6. Cook the fritters for 3-5 minutes per side, or until they are golden brown and cooked through.
7. Transfer the cooked fritters to a plate lined with paper towels to drain any excess oil.
8. Serve the zucchini fritters warm, optionally with a dollop of sour cream, Greek yogurt, or your favorite dipping sauce.
