## Ingredients

- 2 (15-ounce) cans chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and thoroughly dried
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch
- 3 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
- 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 4 cups mixed stir-fry vegetables (e.g., broccoli florets, sliced bell peppers, shredded carrots, snap peas)
- Cooked rice, for serving
- 2 green onions, thinly sliced, for garnish
- 1 teaspoon sesame seeds, for garnish

**For the Stir Fry Sauce:**
- 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon maple syrup or honey
- 1 teaspoon Sriracha (optional, for heat)

## Instructions

1.  In a medium bowl, combine the thoroughly dried chickpeas and cornstarch. Toss until the chickpeas are evenly coated.
2.  Heat 2 tablespoons of sesame oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the coated chickpeas and cook, stirring occasionally, for 8-10 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy. Remove the chickpeas from the pan and set aside.
3.  While the chickpeas are cooking, prepare the stir fry sauce: In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup (or honey), and Sriracha (if using). Set aside.
4.  Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of sesame oil to the same wok or skillet. Add the grated ginger and minced garlic, and stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5.  Add the mixed stir-fry vegetables to the wok. Cook, stirring frequently, for 5-7 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
6.  Return the crispy chickpeas to the wok with the vegetables. Pour the prepared stir fry sauce over everything. Toss gently to coat all ingredients evenly and cook for 1-2 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
7.  Serve immediately over cooked rice, garnished with sliced green onions and sesame seeds.
