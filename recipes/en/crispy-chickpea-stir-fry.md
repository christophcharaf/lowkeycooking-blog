I love a good stir fry, and this Crispy Chickpea Stir Fry is one of my absolute favorites when I'm craving something quick, healthy, and incredibly satisfying. The chickpeas get wonderfully crisp, adding a fantastic texture contrast to the tender-crisp vegetables, all brought together by a flavorful, sticky sauce. It's a weeknight hero in my kitchen, proving that plant-based meals can be just as exciting and hearty as any other.

## Ingredients

- 2 (15-ounce) cans chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and thoroughly patted dry
- 2 tablespoons cornstarch
- 2 tablespoons sesame oil, divided
- 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
- 1 medium head broccoli, cut into small florets
- 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
- 2 carrots, peeled and julienned or thinly sliced
- 1 cup snap peas
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated
- Cooked rice or noodles, for serving (optional)
- Sesame seeds, for garnish
- 2 green onions, sliced, for garnish

**For the Sauce:**
- 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce or tamari
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 2 tablespoons maple syrup or honey
- 1 tablespoon sriracha (or more, to taste)
- 1/4 cup water or vegetable broth
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch

## Instructions

1.  In a medium bowl, toss the thoroughly dried chickpeas with 2 tablespoons of cornstarch until evenly coated.
2.  Heat 1 tablespoon of sesame oil and the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat. Add the coated chickpeas and cook, stirring occasionally, for 8-10 minutes, or until golden brown and crispy. Remove the chickpeas from the pan and set aside.
3.  Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of sesame oil to the wok. Add the broccoli florets, bell pepper, and carrots. Stir fry for 3-4 minutes until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
4.  Add the snap peas, minced garlic, and grated ginger to the wok. Stir fry for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
5.  While the vegetables are cooking, whisk together all sauce ingredients (soy sauce, rice vinegar, maple syrup, sriracha, water, and 1 teaspoon cornstarch) in a small bowl until smooth.
6.  Pour the sauce into the wok with the vegetables. Bring to a simmer, stirring constantly, until the sauce thickens, about 1-2 minutes.
7.  Return the crispy chickpeas to the wok and toss to coat evenly with the sauce and vegetables.
8.  Serve immediately over cooked rice or noodles, if desired. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
