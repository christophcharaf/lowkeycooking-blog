I love finding recipes that are as easy to clean up as they are to make, and this Spiced Chickpea and Spinach Skillet is exactly that! It came about on one of those weeknights when I was too tired to think about complicated meal prep, but still wanted something nourishing and delicious. The warming spices and hearty chickpeas make this surprisingly satisfying, and it’s so forgiving for beginner cooks – you really can’t mess this one up!

## Ingredients

- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 small yellow onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- Pinch of cayenne pepper (optional)
- 1 (15-ounce) can chickpeas, rinsed and drained
- 1 (10-ounce) bag fresh spinach
- 1/4 cup vegetable broth or water
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
- Lemon wedges, for serving (optional)

## Instructions

1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
2. Stir in the minced garlic, cumin, coriander, turmeric, and cayenne pepper (if using). Cook for 1 minute more, until fragrant.
3. Add the rinsed and drained chickpeas to the skillet. Stir to coat them in the spices.
4. Add the fresh spinach, a handful at a time, stirring until it wilts down.
5. Pour in the vegetable broth or water and bring to a simmer. Cook for 2-3 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld and the liquid to reduce slightly.
6. Season generously with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
7. Serve hot, with lemon wedges on the side if desired.
