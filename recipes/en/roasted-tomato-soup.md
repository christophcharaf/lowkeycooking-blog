There's something incredibly comforting about a warm bowl of homemade soup, especially when it's as vibrant and flavorful as this roasted tomato soup. I used to think making soup from scratch was a huge undertaking, but this recipe completely changed my mind. Roasting the tomatoes brings out an amazing depth of flavor that canned soup just can't match, and it's surprisingly simple to achieve. Don't be intimidated, home cooks and starter chefs – this recipe is forgiving, easy to follow, and yields incredibly delicious results.

## Ingredients

- 2 lbs ripe Roma tomatoes, halved
- 1 large yellow onion, roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, peeled
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried basil
- 1/2 tsp dried oregano
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
- 1/2 cup heavy cream (optional, for richness)
- Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

## Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
2. On a large baking sheet, spread out the halved tomatoes, chopped onion, and peeled garlic cloves.
3. Drizzle generously with olive oil, then sprinkle with dried basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Toss gently to coat everything evenly.
4. Roast for 30-35 minutes, or until the tomatoes are softened, slightly caramelized, and the edges are lightly charred.
5. Carefully transfer the roasted vegetables to a large pot or Dutch oven.
6. Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.
7. Using an immersion blender, carefully blend the soup until smooth. Alternatively, transfer in batches to a regular blender (be cautious with hot liquids) and blend until smooth, then return to the pot.
8. Stir in the heavy cream, if using, and continue to simmer for another 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
9. Taste and adjust seasoning with additional salt and pepper as needed.
10. Ladle the hot soup into bowls and garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving.
