There's something incredibly charming about a rustic dessert, and this peach galette is my absolute favorite for showcasing summer's bounty. I love how forgiving and simple it is to make – no fancy pie plates needed, just a free-form crust wrapped around sweet, juicy peaches. It always brings a smile to my face, especially when served warm with a scoop of vanilla ice cream; it reminds me of lazy afternoons and simple pleasures.

## Ingredients

- 1 ¼ cups all-purpose flour
- ½ teaspoon salt
- 8 tablespoons (1 stick) unsalted butter, very cold, cut into small cubes
- ¼ cup ice water, plus more if needed
- 4-5 ripe peaches, peeled, pitted, and sliced ¼-inch thick (about 4 cups)
- ¼ cup granulated sugar, plus 1 tablespoon for sprinkling
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- ½ teaspoon ground cinnamon
- ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
- 1 large egg yolk
- 1 tablespoon milk or water

## Instructions

1.  Make the dough: Whisk flour and salt in a large bowl. Cut in cold butter using a pastry blender or your fingertips until crumbly with pea-sized pieces of butter.
2.  Gradually add ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, mixing until the dough just comes together. Do not overmix. Form into a disk, wrap in plastic, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
3.  Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
4.  Prepare the filling: In a large bowl, gently toss sliced peaches with ¼ cup sugar, cornstarch, cinnamon, nutmeg, and lemon juice.
5.  On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough into a 12-14 inch circle, about ⅛ inch thick. Carefully transfer the dough to the prepared baking sheet.
6.  Arrange the peach filling in the center of the dough, leaving a 2-inch border.
7.  Fold the dough border up and over the peaches, pleating it as you go to create a rustic edge.
8.  Whisk the egg yolk and milk/water for the egg wash. Brush the egg wash over the folded crust. Sprinkle the remaining 1 tablespoon of sugar over the peaches and the crust.
9.  Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the peaches are tender and bubbly.
10. Let the galette cool on the baking sheet for at least 15 minutes before slicing and serving.
